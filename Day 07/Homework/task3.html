elephant

Elephants are the largest living land animals. Three living species are currently recognised:

the African bush elephant (Loxodonta africana), the African forest elephant (L. cyclotis), and

the Asian elephant (Elephas maximus). They are the only surviving members of the family

Elephantidae and the order Proboscidea; extinct relatives include mammoths and mastodons.

Distinctive features of elephants include a long proboscis called a trunk, tusks, large ear flaps.
